Loving More

Love More

So I've been talking to Clara a lot, and she recently told me that I am so patient and loving to her, through everything. And it got me thinking about how some people are more loving and gracious and why others may not be as much. It was a seemingly a random early morning thought, but I was thinking because I don't always have an easy time loving and being patient with people. It's always limited.


Then surprisingly today during church the sermon was on Luke 7:36-50, which basically is the story of a Pharisee and a sinful woman. Jesus says something that was the exact point I was trying to get to when contemplating the reason we love.

"Therefore, I tell you, her many sins have been forgiven - as her great love has shown. But whoever has been forgiven little loves little"

I, as a sinner, have been forgiven of a lot. Realizing that makes it easier for me to love others more and be even more patient with them than I feel like I can handle, because it's what Jesus would do.

I am constantly having to repent and be forgiven, and this leads me to greater love, a love greater and more persistant than mine.

"I am not what I might be, I am not what I ought to be, I am not what I wish to be, I am not what I hope to be. But I thank God I am not what I once was, and I can say with the great apostle, 'By the grace of God I am what I am."' -John Newton
