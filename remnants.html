Remnants

My Grandparents


My family went to Korea during the summer of 2016, our first time in 5 years. Everything had changed. My grandparents were the biggest change that I saw, my memories of the big house down the long road became a significantly smaller house with an even shorter road.

My grandmother wasn't even functional, she was lying in a hospital bed feeding constantly off of the oxygen tank, barely alive. Every few minutes you could hear her muted moan in what sounded like pain.

During our time there, my parents decided that it would be best to take my grandma off ofthe oxygen and if she passes away, then it would be for the best because she was in pain every moment. Not an easy decision for my mom, as my grandmother's youngest child, to make.

I had never done an internal project, but even this time was a bit distant. I don't know my grandparents well at all. I saw them for a couple years of my life and the rest of it was spent away from them. I noticed things I didn't notice that previous time I visited.

The glasses, hmm, I don't remember my grandparents wearing glasses.

The blue sign says something like, elderly care. A crosswalk sign that says watch out for elderly... never seen that before.

They used to have a massive bed in their room I would jump on. That's gone.

My grandma is also gone. Almost normally surreal. I wasn't there when she passed and now I'm abroad, just like before.

Grandpa is still working all the time, that's the same.
