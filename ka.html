KORE-AMERICANS

Kyungmin Cho


I was born in Seoul, South Korea in 1995. My family moved to the United States in 2001, months before the 9/11 terror attack, which plunged the nation into turmoil. The result of that incident was the vast expanse of legislations to hinder immigration to the United States. As an immigrant child, I had never considered my status as an American resident and my parents did everything they could shield me from those worries. My brother and I were blessed with hard working parents and with Americans around us who aided us in becoming naturalized citizens. I realized that I had an easier undocumented upbringing was when I moved to New York City and started to meet people who were undocumented. I would listen to their childhood stories and see how they were living as adults, and it was clearly a more difficult lifestyle than most living in the United States. Most of them had, like me, gotten here at a young age and went to American public schools, learned English, and stood for the Pledge of Allegiance every morning in school.
