Going home

Is going home truly going Home?

Normally when I go back to California (where I grew up) during my school breaks, I feel like I'm a visitor. But this time was significantly different and difficult.

What is the joy in going home?

For me, I go home to see friends and family, the weather is nice and driving is fun, but neither are better than people. This time was different because my parents were still in Maine and my brother left for school as soon as we got back to California. I have a great friend in California, his name is Matt. He has been my longest friend, we've been through a lot together and have both seen our families go through so much change.

Last fall Matt's mother passed away and I was much more impacted than even when my grandmother had passed the year before. *side note please pray for his Dad as he is lonely and sad. Matt's mom, Kati, was like a second mom to me in my early teenage years, such a caring and compasstionate mom. At one point I lived with their family when my family was homeless and she was so welcoming. Kati had the kind of compassion that I long to see in Christians that I meet. So meeting up with basically my only friend in California was so encouraging.

Kati had the kind of compassion that I long to see in Christians that I meet.

I was alone in my parent's new apartment, and decided to stay at Matt's place for some days with his housemates. I felt like I did when I was taking my year off and traveling. Place to place, no home. All the other kids had places to go afterward, but I actually had no home at the time, and I was used to it. Oddly used to it.

I jump into people's lives for months, weeks, or even just days at a time. And I still cherish those relationships I have, even if I see them once a year. My few friends from before university. I try to keep up, and meet up, but it becomes harder and harder.

Matt is strong, hardworking, and creative. He has dreams! And he goes towards his dreams and passions everyday. How encouraging it is.

We have some different views, we disagree on things, but I treat him like a brother and he treats me like a brother. We are family, regardless of religion and politics.

home is only Home if I want it to be.

sam.
