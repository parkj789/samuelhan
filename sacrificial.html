"Letters to People"

To The Sacrificial

Dear Sacrifice,

Talk about something so rewarding but so difficult. As Christians, we often talk about sacrificial love. Sacrifice isn't as easy as we make it sound. We say it's hard but act like it's easy peasy, is it really though? Let's be honest, Sacrifice, you're hard to be friends with. You are the friend everyone has tried to get along with, and the more time I spend with you, the more I realize that it's always going to be hard.

My parents are so fond of you that it almost, ALMOST comes naturally for me to be your friend... or at least WANT to be your friend.

We've gotten to know each other in the past but it's only going to be more difficult, I can feel it. But why you?

I believe that Christians should live intentionally, understanding that actions and words should match and how each affects people. Knowing and believing that what I lose is worth the end reward, to either show love to someone unloved or unseen.

"He is no fool who gives what he cannot keep
to gain what he cannot lose."
- Jim Elliot

Sacrifice comes in many forms, and some are easier than others. Sacrificing your time for someone you love can be easy, but the real sacrifice comes when you have to use your precious time for someone you don't really like and doing it with joy. To be able to see people as God sees them, each and every person without having to hear their heart-breaking testimony. This doesn't come naturally for anyone, but I need to choose to see the precious pearl in each person and learn to show my love to that person.

“But the king replied to Araunah, ‘No, I insist on paying you for it. I will not sacrifice to the Lord my God burnt offerings that cost me nothing.' ”
(2 Samuel 24:24)

Are you sacrificing daily, and what are you sacrificing? Whether it be time, money, food, or energy it shouldn't be free and easy to do. It takes a choice to release comfort and sacrifice something for someone else.

always learning more,


sam.
