A Letter to my Neighbor

Dear Lady Nextdoor

Dear Lady nextdoor,

I'm usually not an anxious person, or even stressed. I have a certain patience and tolerance when it comes to these things. Because I understand where we live. I believe that I should strive for the kind of love and patience that Jesus had. Even without you, I have trouble loving everyone.


Since I've moved in, you have helped me to grow anxious. I'm wary of everything I do in my room, but that sounds odd. Not even being able to be truly comfortable in my own room. Even with this city being infamous for its thin walls. Just like Judy Hopps has to live with in her Zootopia apartment.

But I'm beginning to think that you've gifted me a sort of PTSD. The way that you beat the wall in your frustration instantly gives me anxiety, it makes me unneasy. You are consistent thougg, in that thorough beating on the wall, clearly not using your hand. You must be used to it. You seem to have methodized the way you do it, and it works.

The way I can hear you yelling as if you've stepped on a lego piece, the red rectangular one. It's enough to scare off even the worst, Mr. Screwtape himself. And how I can vaguely make out the words "you chink" is, again, consistent.

"And how I can vaguely make out the words "you chink" is, again, consistent."

Even that one time, remember?


I was recording a voice message to send to my girlfriend. Just reading her Matthew Chapter 12 and I stopped midway, just to let you continue in silence, or rather, break the silence.


Yes, I am a chink, something I can't help. But I wish I could meet you, see what you're really like. We could even mutually agree to respect each other. To tell you that I'm just a college senior trying to finish his thesis on being a chink in America. After all, we live in a small city of 10 million. Anxiety at home is something neither of us want.


Now I know, I'm no Wormwood, no demon-in-training and I am not out to ruin your home life. So please, don't try to ruin mine.


sam.
