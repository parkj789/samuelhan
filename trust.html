Trust.

Trust.

It's something weird. Hard for some people and easy for some. Trust for me is easily given, and almost hard to lose. But once it's lost...

Over the past several years my trust has been truly tested, and I don't believe it'll stop. It is difficult to put my full trust into someone or something, or at least it should be. When I drive, I'm trusting myself and every single person around me to follow the law. When I bike, I do the same. But its much more different to put trust like that into strangers that I may never meet, than to put my trust in a friend. Relationships have a way like that. It is harder, requires commitment, and involves much more risk. We put ourselves at rist to trust someone we love.

I don't have much trouble trusting people, but I have learned that many do not feel the same. Maybe it was the way I grew up? Maybe I've never had anyone break my trust? (No, definitely not that one) It can be many things, but at the core of it, I have a strong conviction when I shouldn't completely give my trust. Because of someone I should be wary about.

I try not to be reckless in giving my trust, but gracious.
Not stingy, but cautious.

Even still, God throws some weird situations at me that make me rethink the way that I live life, maybe the way that I trust. God's trust in us was 100%, and this trust was given to us knowing that we would fail, again and again. He is teaching me consistency, repetition in my grace. Grace shown to myself and those around me, regardless of what we have done. I causes me to go beyond my "comfort zone", always. Even as my little "comfort zone" expands, he asks that I extend it more and more. As we have been given, so shall we give. Freely.

This is not to say that people won't anger or frustrate you, that is inevitable. But forgive and love.

"I will heal their waywardness and love them freely, for my anger has turned away from them"
- Hosea 14:4
