Korean Enough

Korean Enough


This short film "Korean Enough" is all about being a multicultural individual in America. It focuses on the stories of 5 individuals who speak on personal experiences of living in America as an ethnically Korean person.
I personally prep and cook the Korean meals as a tool to create conversations that transcend the cuisine itself and focus on topics such as traumatic experiences, cultural differences, and a deeper look into Korean culture.
Food and culture go together, but when you grow up with multiple cultures, things get blurred and confusing. Everyone of multiple ethnicities have to go through this identity crisis at one point and most will need help working through it, I’m trying to open a conversation. Not just for Korean-Americans but for anyone willing to talk about and respect the cultures they want to discuss. It’s about trying to be respectful of both cultures and embrace them both at the same time, without prejudice and assumption.
