"Letters to People"

To The Comfortable

Dear Comfort,

We love you. Please come back to visit as often as possible. The feeling you give must have come from God, so therefore come again. I'm sure God will stop you from coming if it was his will.

I didn't grow up knowing you, but I saw you all around me. People at my school, church, and neighborhood all knew you. You were like the mutual friend that everyone knew and said, "You have to meet him, you would love him!"

Now I've become aquaintances with you over the past 4 years. We've met a couple of times and I've almost forgotten about a friend promised to me.

"Blessed are the poor in spirit,
for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." -Matthew 5:3

Being poor isn't a "strong suit", actually it's supposed to be the opposite. Most of the world is pushing for strength, independence, and beauty, when really we should be pushing for weakness, dependence, and ugliness. We all have it, we're just getting better at hiding it. The more we say it, the more we begin to believe that lie about us. We're always glorifying someone, but when we embrace the fact of being compared to Him, we're glorifying someone perfect, humility is a side outcome.

Comfort, you are the most social of butterflies. The one that everyone wants to be friends with, even the cool kids. You appeal to everyone and are at the heart of so many dreams and goals. I haven't been so close with Poverty, but I hear he keeps a small but good group of friends. They are (in a worldy sense) a weak, ugly, and dependent group.

I have fond memories of Poverty, all throughout my life, though he isn't really the focus. The focus is where the dependence comes in, Jesus, our best friend. Jesus reminds us to keep our flavor as Salt and to burn as the Light.

I don't want to say it, but I hope we don't run into each other until I see Jesus face to face. I'm sure I can welcome you then, when Poverty is no more.

In search of better,


sam.
