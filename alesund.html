Ålesund

Ålesund and Alnes.


I spent a week in Ålesund to be with and teach some of the students on the Creative Arts DTS in YWAM Ålesund. I've actually been to Ålesund before, for a single day. I didn't get to do or see much because I had come to do a studio shoot for a brand. It's a beautiful town.

From brisk walks on top of the Aksla viewpoint to an icy hike in Alnes with breathtaking views of the small town and ocean, what I've most enjoyed is to get to know everyone. To challenge people's creativity (including mine), inspire and be inspired. I've missed being in a creative community, where there are musicians, fine artists, photographers, filmmakers, actors, and dancers.

What I make, I make for God but it needs to be seen.

How much more meaningful it is to go to places where I can actually invest in the people, make friends, and see God's beauty with them. This trip would be so so boring by myself. That's something I've been realizing more and more is that although I'm introverted, I want to do life with people. The essence of my gifting is founded on being seen. What I make, I make for God but it needs to be seen!

My time in Ålesund was short, and I started to feel at home on Sunday, the day before I left. I'm excited to keep up with the students to see how their art progresses and see how they grow in it. Embracing the fact that they are artists, and finding out what kind of artist they want to be.

Ålesund has charm

With it's art and its beauty

Ålesund is art


"Ålesund has charm" Haiku by yours truly.
